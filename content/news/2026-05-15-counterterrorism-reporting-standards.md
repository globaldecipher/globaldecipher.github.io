## Context

Terrorism reporting often moves through stages. First comes an alert, then unofficial claims, then competing figures, and finally a clearer picture. When those stages collapse into a single headline, audiences lose the ability to judge what is known and what remains uncertain.

## TGD standard

The Global Decipher will label material as claim, confirmation, assessment, or correction. This is simple, but it matters. A claim tells readers what an actor says. Confirmation tells readers what can be independently supported.

## Editorial posture

TGD will avoid publishing operational details that could endanger civilians, responders, or ongoing security work. Public interest reporting does not require amplifying tactical information or extremist media products.
