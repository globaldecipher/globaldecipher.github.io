A note on the columns. **Incidents** is the count of distinct events that surfaced in public reporting. **Claimed** is the subset for which a militant claim was made publicly. **Verified** is the subset where the claim has been corroborated by at least one independent source (state acknowledgement, local journalism, or two converging open sources).

## What changed in May

- TTP-linked channels increased rhythm of public statements after mid-month operations in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa.
- IS-K provincial outlets pushed Crocus-era retrospectives to coincide with court proceedings against [Mohammad Sharifullah](/profiles/mohammad-sharifullah/).
- A measurable drop in casualty-figure-only claims (claims without operational context) — the second consecutive month of that pattern.

## What to watch in June

> Public-source monitoring is a leading indicator of attention, not of capability. The two diverge often. Where they converge — sustained claim activity matched by independent corroboration — is where the desk concentrates its scarce verification time.

- Whether IS-K media output normalises after the Sharifullah verdict
- Whether TTP claim patterns continue tracking with cross-border narrative cycles
- Whether the casualty-figure-only category re-emerges as state communication tightens

## Methodology in brief

The desk uses public sources only. Single-source claims are labelled as such. Casualty figures are not treated as verified until they appear in independent reporting or official statements. See [Methodology](/methodology/) for the full standard.
