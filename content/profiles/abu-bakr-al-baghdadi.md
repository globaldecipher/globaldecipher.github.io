## Status

Deceased. Killed during a U.S. operation in Syria in October 2019.

## Identification

- **Name:** Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi
- **Born:** Iraq
- **Organisation:** Islamic State
- **Role:** Leader of Islamic State from 2010 to 2019

## Background

Baghdadi was publicly reported as a graduate-level religious scholar before he became globally notorious. He completed advanced study in Islamic studies and was also reported to have spent time editing a medieval manuscript on Qur'anic recitation.

Another important formative detail is his detention at Camp Bucca in 2004. He was released the same year and later re-emerged inside the insurgent-jihadist ecosystem that produced Islamic State.

## Significance

Baghdadi's importance lies in how he fused claimed religious legitimacy, insurgent succession politics, and the projection of a "caliphate" brand across Iraq, Syria, and far-flung affiliates. His leadership turned Islamic State from a regional insurgent movement into a transnational militant project with global recruitment and affiliate structures.
