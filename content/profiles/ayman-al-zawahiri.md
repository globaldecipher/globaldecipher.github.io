## Status

Deceased. Killed by a U.S. Central Intelligence Agency drone strike in the Sherpur district of Kabul, Afghanistan, on July 31, 2022, in a safehouse reportedly linked to a senior aide of Sirajuddin Haqqani.

## Identification

- **Full name:** Ayman Mohammed Rabie al-Zawahiri
- **Born:** June 19, 1951, Maadi, Cairo, Egypt
- **Nationality:** Egyptian
- **Organisations:** Egyptian Islamic Jihad — leader (1991–2001); al-Qaeda — second general emir (June 2011 – July 2022)

## Background

Trained as a surgeon at Cairo University. Drawn into Islamist organising as a teenager and imprisoned from 1981 to 1984 for peripheral involvement in the assassination of Egyptian president Anwar Sadat. Released, he rebuilt Egyptian Islamic Jihad through the late 1980s and 1990s, fled to Sudan with bin Laden after Saudi expulsion, and relocated to Afghanistan. He formally merged Egyptian Islamic Jihad into al-Qaeda in 2001 and served as bin Laden's deputy through the September 11 era.

## Significance

Al-Zawahiri is widely treated as the chief ideological and organisational architect of al-Qaeda's post-1998 doctrine — including the rationale for prioritising attacks on the United States. He authored *Knights Under the Prophet's Banner* and a stream of recorded statements that shaped al-Qaeda's affiliate model and its rivalry with the Islamic State. As emir from 2011 to 2022 he presided over a network that had become more fragmented but expanded across the Sahel, Horn of Africa, Arabian Peninsula, and South Asia.
