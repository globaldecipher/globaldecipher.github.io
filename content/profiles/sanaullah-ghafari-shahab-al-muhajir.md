## Status

Public reporting on exact current whereabouts remains uncertain. High-confidence public profiles reviewed in the TGD actor database report identify him as ISIS-K's leader appointed by Islamic State core in 2020.

## Identification

- **Name:** Sanaullah Ghafari
- **Alias:** Shahab al-Muhajir
- **Organisation:** Islamic State Khorasan Province (ISIS-K)
- **Role:** Leader of ISIS-K

## Background

Ghafari is associated in public reporting with ISIS-K's shift from a mainly regional insurgent actor toward a branch capable of plotting or inspiring attacks far beyond Afghanistan.

Reuters reporting cited in the TGD actor database report credits him with helping transform the branch's external posture, including operations linked to the Kabul airport bombing, the Iran bombings, and the Moscow concert-hall assault.

## Significance

The underused organisational detail is recruitment composition. Public reporting and specialist analysis stress ISIS-K's aggressive cultivation of transnational and non-Pashtun networks, especially Tajik militants, under his tenure. That helps explain the branch's broader external posture.
