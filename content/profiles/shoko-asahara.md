## Status

Deceased. Executed in Japan in 2018.

## Identification

- **Name:** Shoko Asahara
- **Birth name:** Chizuo Matsumoto
- **Organisation:** AUM Shinrikyo
- **Role:** Founder and cult leader

## Background

Asahara created AUM Shinrikyo as a millenarian movement that fused apocalyptic religion with techno-utopian ambition. The group became globally notorious for the Tokyo subway sarin attack.

Public reference material records his central role and the later continuation of the movement under successor branding, including Aleph.

## Significance

Asahara's organisational afterlife is central to his profile. His execution did not end concern around successor groups. Reporting has noted that his remains remained legally disputed because Japanese authorities feared relic-like veneration by followers, while successor groups continued to attract adherents and remain under surveillance.
