## Status

Active. The U.S. State Department designated the Balochistan Liberation Army as a Foreign Terrorist Organization in August 2025, after an earlier U.S. terrorism designation in 2019. Pakistan also treats the group as proscribed.

## Identification

- **Name:** Balochistan Liberation Army
- **Common abbreviation:** BLA
- **Also known as:** Baloch Liberation Army
- **Organisation type:** Baloch ethnonationalist separatist militant organisation
- **Primary operating area:** Balochistan, with reach into other parts of Pakistan

## Background

The National Counterterrorism Center describes the BLA as a secular ethnonationalist separatist group based in Pakistan and one of the most capable militant organisations in South Asia. Its stated objective is an independent Balochistan.

The group has expanded its operating area and attack profile over time, with activity concentrated in Balochistan and spillover capacity into other provinces. Public-source reporting and official profiles associate the group with attacks against Pakistani security services, infrastructure, and Chinese-linked interests.

TGD's April 2026 assessment noted BLA activity in Balochistan, including a maritime-facing incident near Jiwani and the group's attempt to signal a wider coastal operating concept.

## Significance

BLA matters for the desk because it represents a different conflict ecosystem from the KP-centered TTP threat picture. Its activity is tied to separatist militancy, Balochistan's geography, transport routes, and pressure on state mobility and strategic infrastructure.

The May 18, 2026 TGD feed also carried an update on a multi-day security operation near Quetta, citing official provincial comments that three commanders of Balochistan-based militant groups had been taken into custody and 35 operatives killed during recent intelligence-based operations. That update should be tracked carefully, because Balochistan IBO reporting often changes as official details, affiliations, and casualty figures are clarified.
