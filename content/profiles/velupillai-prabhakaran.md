## Status

Deceased. Killed in May 2009 during the final stage of the Sri Lankan civil war.

## Identification

- **Name:** Velupillai Prabhakaran
- **Born:** Valvettithurai, Sri Lanka
- **Organisation:** Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE)
- **Role:** Founder and leader

## Background

Prabhakaran entered armed militancy in 1972 through the Tamil New Tigers and reconstituted the movement as the LTTE in 1976. Public reporting described him as the son of a government employee from Valvettithurai.

Under his leadership, the LTTE developed into one of the most sophisticated insurgent organisations of its era, combining battlefield operations with internal security, political control, suicide operations, and assassination.

## Significance

Prabhakaran built a movement that functioned not only as an insurgent group but also as a quasi-state structure in areas under its control. The LTTE's no-surrender culture was central to its identity; end-stage reporting noted that Prabhakaran reportedly wore a cyanide capsule intended for use if capture became imminent.
