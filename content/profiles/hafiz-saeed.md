## Status

In Pakistani custody according to the UN summary cited in the TGD actor database report. The same summary states he has been serving a 78-year sentence since February 2020; Reuters separately reported an additional 31-year sentence in 2022.

## Identification

- **Name:** Hafiz Saeed
- **Organisation:** Lashkar-e-Tayyiba (LeT) / Jamaat-ud-Dawa ecosystem
- **Role:** Senior leader, fundraiser, and organiser
- **Operating theatre:** South Asia

## Background

Saeed is often reduced to a public face, but the updated UN narrative makes his role more operational than that shorthand suggests. It describes involvement in LeT leadership, fundraising, camp management, and overseas facilitation.

The same narrative says he travelled to Afghanistan during the late 1970s or early 1980s for militant training and came into contact with Abdullah Azzam.

## Significance

Saeed's significance lies in the bridge between public-facing mobilisation, institutional fundraising, and militant facilitation. The UN narrative also says he helped organise LeT movement into Iraq and established a LeT office in Quetta to assist the Taliban.
