## Status

Deceased. Executed in 2001.

## Identification

- **Name:** Timothy McVeigh
- **Nationality:** American
- **Role:** Perpetrator of the Oklahoma City bombing
- **Ideological category:** Domestic anti-government terrorism

## Background

McVeigh was a Gulf War veteran radicalised by anti-government grievances, including Waco and Ruby Ridge. On April 19, 1995, he carried out the Oklahoma City bombing, which killed 168 people.

He remains one of the clearest cases of anti-government terrorism not dependent on a foreign insurgent organisation.

## Significance

For database design, McVeigh belongs in a domestic anti-state terrorist category rather than a generic extremist bucket. His case also shows the limits of hidden-mastermind mythology: the state he hated captured him quickly and secured convictions through a fast-moving investigation.
