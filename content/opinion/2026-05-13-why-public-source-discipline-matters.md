## Opinion

Terrorism research has a visibility problem. When analysis becomes too slow, public narratives are shaped by rumor. When analysis becomes too fast, it risks becoming an amplifier for actors who want attention.

The answer is not silence. The answer is discipline.

Public-source researchers should show what category of information they are using, what confidence level they assign, and where uncertainty remains. The audience should never have to guess whether a sentence is a fact, a claim, or an assessment.

## TGD position

The Global Decipher will treat credibility as a product feature. Every briefing should help readers understand not only what happened, but how we know what we think we know.
